# Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics – A Modern Teaching Course Constitution
<!-- This constitution defines the core principles, structure, and governance for the book project. -->

## Core Principles

### I. Content Quality & Originality
Produce original, accurate, and high-quality educational content. All content must be fact-checked and technically correct.

### II. Structured Academic Style
Maintain a structured academic style with clear concepts, diagrams (described in text), examples, and case studies.

### III. Real-World Relevance
Prioritize real-world robotics, AI hardware, embodiment, control systems, sensors, actuators, and human-robot interaction.

### IV. Accessible & Deep Learning
Use simple language for beginners but include advanced depth for higher-level learners.

### V. Ethical & Safe Robotics
Follow safe and responsible robotics principles. No harmful or dangerous robotics instructions.

## Formatting & Structure

The final book must be created using Docusaurus.
All chapters and content must be written as Markdown inside the `/docs` folder.
Provide a clear Table of Contents and section hierarchy.
Include practical learning outcomes and short review questions in each chapter.

## Tone & Audience

Target audience: Students and beginners in robotics & AI.
Educational, encouraging, and well-organized.

## Governance

This constitution supersedes all other practices for the book project.
Amendments require documentation, approval, and a migration plan if content structure is affected.
All content must comply with ethical and safety guidelines for robotics.
